Divine Family of Shree Raghunath Temple

Shree Raghunath Temple is home to a sacred family of deities worshipped in the Sanatan Hindu tradition. Each deity is honored with daily aartis, special abhishekams, festival celebrations and personal sponsorship opportunities. Click any deity below to read more.

Presiding Deities

Shree Ram Parivaarॐ श्री राम जय राम जय जय राम

The presiding deities of Shree Raghunath Temple — Bhagwan Shree Ram, Mata Sita, Lakshman Ji and Shree Hanuman Ji. Bhagwan Ram, the seventh avatar of Vishnu, is revered as Maryada Purushottam — the ideal man whose life of righteousness, compassion and dharma is enshrined in the Ramayana. The temple takes its name from Raghunath ji, Lord of the Raghu dynasty.

The Eternal Devotee

Hanuman Jiॐ हं हनुमते नमः

Shree Hanuman — the embodiment of devotion, strength and selfless service — is the eternal devotee of Bhagwan Ram. Worshipped as Sankat Mochan, the remover of obstacles, he is invoked for courage, protection and unwavering bhakti. The temple holds Hanuman Chalisa & Bhajans every Tuesday at 6:30 PM.

The Hanuman Chalisa — forty chaupais composed in Awadhi by the 16th-century poet-saint Tulsidas — is one of the most beloved devotional hymns in the Hindu tradition and is recited weekly at the temple.

Lord of the Universe

Shree Jagannatha Parivaarजगन्नाथः स्वामी नयन-पथ-गामी भवतु मे

The sacred trio of Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra and Devi Subhadra — venerated in the great tradition of Puri Dham. Jagannath, the "Lord of the Universe," is a form of Vishnu/Krishna and embodies cosmic compassion that transcends caste and creed. Every year the temple hosts a grand Ratha Yatra, where the deities ride upon decorated chariots through the community.

Lord of the Seven Hills

Shree Balaji Parivaarॐ नमो वेंकटेशाय

Bhagwan Venkateshwara (Balaji) with Sridevi and Bhudevi — the supremely compassionate form of Lord Vishnu of Tirumala. Devotees seek his blessings for prosperity, dharma, and relief from worldly burdens. The temple performs a grand Monthly Shree Balaji Parivaar Abhishekam on the 3rd Saturday of every month at 10:00 AM.

The Preserver

Vishnu Bhagwanॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय

Bhagwan Vishnu — the cosmic preserver, who incarnates in every age (Ram, Krishna, Narasimha, Vamana and others) to restore dharma. Worshipped at the temple in his various forms, especially through the monthly Satyanarayan Katha and the annual Ratha Yatra.

The Auspicious One

Shiv Ji — Mahadevॐ नमः शिवाय

Bhagwan Shiva — Mahadev, the supreme yogi and lord of compassion. Worshipped at the temple with abhishekam, bilva patra and Rudram chanting. The temple conducts Shiva Abhishekam on Pradosham (6:30 PM) and grand celebrations on Maha Shivratri. The Shiv Puran Katha is held periodically.

Yogeshwar

Shree Krishnaहरे कृष्ण हरे कृष्ण कृष्ण कृष्ण हरे हरे

Bhagwan Shree Krishna — the eighth avatar of Vishnu, the divine flutist of Vrindavan, charioteer of Arjuna and the speaker of the Bhagavad Gita. His leelas are celebrated with devotion through bhajans, abhishekam and the grand Janmashtami midnight celebration. The temple also observes Govardhan Puja and Radha Ashtami.

Shree Ganeshaॐ गं गणपतये नमः

Bhagwan Ganesha — the elephant-headed lord of new beginnings, wisdom and the remover of obstacles. By tradition he is the first deity invoked in every Hindu puja. The temple celebrates a grand Shree Ganesh Mahotsav annually, with ten days of pujas, bhajans and the joyous visarjan procession.

Bhog Offering: Devotees can sponsor ladoos to be offered to Ganesh ji during the Mahotsav (minimum 11–21 laddus per day). Sponsorship tiers include Maha Yajmaan, Visarjan Yajmaan, Yajmaan, Altar Puja, Anna-Daan and Decoration. Kindly contact the temple front desk or Vani ji for details.

Adishakti

Maa Durgaॐ ऐं ह्रीं क्लीं चामुण्डायै विच्चे

Maa Durga — the supreme Mother Goddess, embodiment of cosmic shakti who slays evil and protects dharma. Worshipped daily with Durga Saptashati paath and celebrated with grand Chaitra and Sharad Navratri (twice a year) — nine nights of garba, bhajans, kanya puja and Devi pujan.

Vastra Sponsorship: Devotees may sponsor sarees / vastra for Maa Durga. Kindly inquire at the temple front desk.

Goddess of Wealth & Grace

Maa Lakshmiॐ श्रीं महालक्ष्म्यै नमः

Mahalakshmi — divine consort of Bhagwan Vishnu and goddess of prosperity, fortune, beauty and grace. She bestows both laukik (worldly) and paaramarthik (spiritual) wealth. Worshipped at the temple with special Lakshmi Puja on Fridays, Purnima, and during the grand Diwali celebrations.

Goddess of Wisdom & Arts

Maa Saraswatiॐ ऐं सरस्वत्यै नमः

Maa Saraswati — the goddess of knowledge, learning, music and the fine arts. Seated upon a white lotus, with veena in hand, she is the source of all wisdom. Worshipped especially on Vasant Panchami and during Navratri. Children of Raghukul Vidyapeeth invoke her blessings before every term.

The Sun Deity

Surya Bhagwanॐ सूर्याय नमः

Surya Bhagwan — the radiant Sun deity, source of life, health and vitality. Worshipped through Surya Namaskar, the recitation of Aditya Hridayam Stotra and Arghya offered at sunrise. Specially honored on Sundays, Rath Saptami, and Chhath Puja.

Monthly Sacred Katha

Satyanarayan Kathaॐ सत्यनारायणाय नमः

The Satyanarayan Vrat & Katha is a beloved puja dedicated to Bhagwan Vishnu in his form as the Lord of Truth. Held every month on Purnima (full moon), this auspicious puja brings peace, prosperity and family well-being to all who sponsor and participate. The katha is open to all devotees, who are encouraged to bring family and friends.
